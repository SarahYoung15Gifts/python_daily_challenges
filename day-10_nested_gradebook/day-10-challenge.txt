The Scenario:
In the real world, data isn't just a list of strings; it's often a "list of dictionaries" (similar to how a database or a JSON API might send you information). You are a teacher managing a small classroom, and you need to calculate the average grade for each student.

Your Task:

Use this data structure:
students = [
    {"name": "Alice", "grades": [85, 90, 92]},
    {"name": "Bob", "grades": [70, 68, 72]},
    {"name": "Charlie", "grades": [100, 95, 98]}
]

Loop through the list of students.

For each student, calculate their average grade.

Print a summary in this format:
Alice: Average = 89.0

Identify the "Star Student": Keep track of who has the highest average and print their name at the end: "Star Student: Charlie".
